Nero

Exceed / EX Act / MAX Act

Nero can charge up his Red Queen sword as if it were a motorbike, increasing his attack power and speed of his attacks, which can also instantly charge one bar by pressing the charge button at the right time (or, if it’s at the perfect moment, fully charged). Every attack done uses 1 bar of the Exceed charge, excluding some, which use all of them. These excluded moves are:

  • EX Streak
  • EX High Roller
  • EX Payline
  • EX Calibur
  • EX Shuffle

Devil Breakers

Nero has access to a wide variety of special toys, one that vibrates the fucking electro-magnetic field, and one of which... just vibrates. He can use these Breakers in a variety of ways, be it attacking, dodging, or, in the case of the vibrator, healing by jerking it off (actual line in the moves description). All of these Breakers have a reasoning behind why they’re called Devil Breakers. They break on one of 3 conditions - you get hit while in the middle of one of their moves, you use Breakage, or, you activate their special ultimate super deluxe move by holding down the style button until the Breaker glows. Nero can hold a max of 8 Breakers at any given time, and they’re scattered around (most) levels if needed.

Dante

Sin Devil Trigger

After attempting to kill himself merge his human half with his demon half, Dante unlocks his SDT (NOT STD, VERY DIFFERENT THINGS). This is a form which, normally, is all or nothing - that is, you use the full bar, or don’t use the form. For Dante, it takes 12 bars of Devil Trigger to fully charge it up. During this state, Dante cannot use any other weapon than Devil Sword Dante, and the moveset is completely changed. The only way to extend the usage of this form to either buy the Trigger Heart ability, or to use the Quad S ability. The Quad S ability activates when you are at SSS Rank for style, and then going into SDT (hence, Quad S - SSS + SDT). It gives you a brief period where you can exit at no cost to the bar, but, if you do not exit during that time, it will return to normal. This is shown as a form of pure power, and brings Dante closer to Vergil’s beliefs - power is absolute.

Styles

Dante has access to 4 unique styles - Trickster, Swordmaster, Gunslinger and Royalguard. I go over them briefly in his Devil Trigger section, however, this section is more dedicated to them. When you’re not in DT and/or don’t have DSD’s floating swords around you, these effects are weaker and more limited - except for Royalguard, where the swords don’t directly enhance the effect. I will go over each effect in their own chapter.

Trickster

The easiest to learn of the four styles, this style allows for faster movement - and provides invincibility frames during use. The style can be used both mid-air and on the ground, and is somewhat reminiscent of Vergil’s dodge move, as is with a lot of their moves. Dante is able to dash to any direction he wants, however, once he dashes once in mid-air, he begins descending, which can be reset by jumping on an enemy (as with Air Hike), and is also able to teleport to the enemy (slightly in air with basic input) by locking on and holding forward while using the style button. This teleport can be enhanced by doing Back->Forward + Style instead of just holding forward, placing you on the ground instead of in air. While Devil Sword Dante’s swords float around you, Trickster is enhanced for faster dodges and for more air dashes (2-3 extra) or air teleports.

Swordmaster

Swordmaster is a slightly more complicated style to learn, however, is still quite easy. In all situations, it provides the user with more moves to use - be it air attacks for Rebellion or DSS, extra combos for Cavaliere, or access to the Fire Moveset for King Cerberus. A majority of these moves are for combo extenders (Rebellion/DSS), combo finishers (King Cerberus), or just hard hitting moves (Cavaliere), however, can also be Jump-Cancelled out of to extend these further, as with almost any move. With DSD, you’re able to use the spectral swords on it, giving access to imitations of multiple moves, and the only move DSD kept from DSS and Rebellion in Swordmaster - Dance Macabre. When you have DSD’s spectral swords floating around you from Swords Formation, or Devil Trigger while DSD is equipped, the swords will follow and attack alongside your attacks, with DSD being the most accurate with these hits.

Gunslinger

Gunslinger is probably one of my favourite styles, after Swordmaster. It is also the BEST combo extender style that Dante has. With Dual Kalina Ann, Dante can relaunch enemies, with Dr Faust, Dante can deal even more than he already does with the weapon (without even attacking), with Shotgun, you get a second pair of nunchucks to use (but with bullets). Other than just being a great combo extender, Gunslinger also makes weapons that might not be too good pretty damn decent and/or fun to use (see: Shotgun, where it combines Trickster with the gun shots). With DSD’s swords, they shoot at anything that is a threat (such as Vergi’s Summon Swords in his Boss Fight), knocking small enemies over, and dealing a decent amount of damage.

Royalguard

The “git gud, schum” style. If you hit it right, you block all damage at no cost. If you hold the button/tap too early, you block all damage at the cost of DT. If you use Lock-On + Back + STYLE and hold it, you block all damage and hit the enemy back (very useful for Furries, I mean Furies). With DSD’s swords, Royalguard will ALWAYS block some damage, even if you’re fucking stupid and don’t even tap the button. It, uh, doesn’t help for Hell and Hell, but it does work.

Vergil (character)

Concentration

The concentration meter, Vergil’s only unique mechanic (with SDT being a shared mechanic of Dante), and one that does a surprising amount for something that is barely talked about. With Yamato, the attacks reach further, with Mirage Edge, the attacks hit more often, and with Beowulf, almost every attack can be charged to a third stage. Concentration is also a requirement for all Super Moves - technically excluding World of V, but for it to actually do something, you need Level 1 Concentration. Every weapon has it’s own Super Move (Yamato technically having two!), all related to one of it’s abilities. Yamato has Judgement Cut End - a VASTLY upgraded form of Judgement Cut, dealing similar damage at every range of the attack - and, World of V, if you count Vergil stabbing himself with the Yamato as being a Yamato Super Move - which heals the user on use, for varying amounts depending on Concentration level, and dealing damage (but not killing anything till the end), generating more Green Orbs for any killed enemy, during which, Vergil is also immune to all damage. Mirage Edge has Deep Stinger, pretty much Dante’s SDT Stinger move, but much stronger, not much more I can add on to that. Finally, Beowulf has Hell on Earth, the user puts their fucking spirit into one punch, charges it up (by looks of it, to Level 3 due to the purple glow at the peak), then smashes the ground. It deals most damage at close range, and almost nothing at long range.

Sin Devil Trigger

Vergil’s Sin Devil Trigger, on the surface, acts very similarly to Dante’s, requiring the bar to be charged to the max to be used. However, it is when you enter the form that changes are noticeably drastic. Unlike Dante, Vergil has access to all 3 of his weapons, and their original movesets - albeit, with moves added on, and with quite a few moves/combos slightly altered. It also enhances his speed, and his roll is changed to a dash to the side/dash to the back, both moves that can be done outside of the form, albeit with more inputs (Triangle + Lock-On + L Stick to the side + X / Lock-On + Back->Foward + Circle). All 3 of his weapons gain a Super Move, similar to Dante’s Judgement in his SDT. However, unlike Dante’s Judgement, Vergil is able to use these moves in his human form (downside being that the full SDT bar is used and not just a quarter).

Shared

Devil Trigger

Nero / Dante

Nero and Dante share the same version of a DT, you activate it, you change forms, and you become more powerful. In the case of Nero, his Buster moves (IN SOME CASES) become stronger, and he gains spinning spectral swords whenever he shoots his gun - reminiscent of Vergil’s summon swords. Dante, however, uses his DT for his Styles, and for certain moves. When he uses Stinger (DSD, DSS or Rebellion), he loses a bar or two of DT, and the same is true for Redline, however, the moves gain a LOT of power in return. When using each style, Dante also gains certain buffs when DSD is equipped - Trickster gives him faster dashes, and 4 in the air instead of 1, due to the 4 spectral swords floating behind him; Gunslinger shoots the spectral swords at “Threats”, such as enemies, or, in the case of the Vergil boss battle, Vergil’s summoned swords; Swordmaster has the swords attack alongside whatever attack Dante is doing at the time, being much more accurate to the sword when DSD is being used, where they merge back into the sword; and, finally, Royalguard has them used as a shield to limit damage if you fail to guard, or you just fail a guard.

V

V uses his Devil Trigger as a way to control the beast called Nightmare, or, as a way to allow his summons to attack on their own - practically just Auto Mode without the debuff, even the devs didn’t like playing him. While Nightmare is out, his white hair shows, and his summons are immortal (or revived if killed). V can also directly control Nightmare by hopping on his back, slowing the DT drain, however, treating the DT as Nightmare’s health bar (as what Shadow and Griffon have). Other than that, there’s not really anything with V’s DT - he gains no health regen from it, however, he does get more green orbs in general.

Vergil

Vergil’s DT is one of the more interesting situations. Due to his beliefs for absolute power, a popular theory is that he changed his normal DT to his new Doppleganger after unlocking his Sin Devil Trigger - going all out from the start, instead of slowly increasing his power like Dante, or perhaps Nero, would do, to toy with their enemy. His Doppleganger is always locked to the Yamato, allowing for some interesting combos if you know what you’re doing, and can’t replicate Judgement Cut End. If Vergil goes into SDT, the Doppleganger’s moveset will change to match the form change. The Doppleganger also has 3 speeds - default, slow, and fast. These speeds affect the rate at which DT will be used as well - slow uses it slowly, fast uses it faster, and default uses it at the normal rate. No matter what speed you choose, any Perfect Judgement Cut will come out at the same speed as Vergil, with the teleport also being at the same time.
